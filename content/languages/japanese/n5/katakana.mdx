## What is Katakana?

**Katakana** (片仮名) is the second Japanese alphabet. Like hiragana, each character represents a syllable — and the sounds are identical. However, katakana has angular, sharper strokes compared to hiragana's rounded shapes.

Katakana is used for:

- **Foreign loanwords** (外来語, gairaigo): コーヒー (koohii — coffee), テレビ (terebi — TV)
- **Foreign names**: アメリカ (Amerika — America), マリア (Maria)
- **Scientific names** and technical jargon
- **Onomatopoeia** and sound effects in manga
- **Emphasis** — like italics in English

> A huge portion of Japanese vocabulary comes from English. Recognizing katakana unlocks hundreds of words instantly.

## Hiragana vs Katakana Comparison

Some characters look similar, others are completely different:

| Sound | Hiragana | Katakana | Notes |
|-------|----------|----------|-------|
| a | あ | ア | Very different |
| i | い | イ | Similar vertical lines |
| u | う | ウ | Slightly similar |
| e | え | エ | Very different |
| o | お | オ | Different |
| ka | か | カ | Similar structure |
| ki | き | キ | Different |
| ku | く | ク | Similar |
| ko | こ | コ | Very similar |
| shi | し | シ | Mirror-like |
| tsu | つ | ツ | Easy to confuse with シ |
| n | ん | ン | Easy to confuse with ソ |

## Long Vowels in Katakana

Katakana uses **ー** (a long dash) to extend vowel sounds, while hiragana uses extra vowel characters:

- コーヒー (koohii) — the ー extends the preceding vowel
- スーパー (suupaa) — supermarket

## Common Confusions

The most frequently confused katakana pairs:

- **シ (shi) vs ツ (tsu)** — シ has two dots on the left side; ツ has two dots on the right
- **ン (n) vs ソ (so)** — ン is more vertical; ソ is more diagonal
- **ウ (u) vs ヲ (wo)** — ウ has a simple top; ヲ has a longer horizontal bar
