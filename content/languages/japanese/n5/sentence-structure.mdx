## Japanese Word Order: SOV

English uses **SVO** order: **S**ubject → **V**erb → **O**bject.

> "I eat sushi." (S=I, V=eat, O=sushi)

Japanese uses **SOV** order: **S**ubject → **O**bject → **V**erb. **The verb always comes at the end.**

> わたしは すしを たべます。
> I (topic) sushi (object) eat.

This is the single most important structural difference to internalize.

## The Copula: です (desu)

**です** (desu) is the polite form of "to be" — it links a subject to a noun or adjective.

> Pattern: [Topic] は [Noun/Adjective] **です**。

| Japanese | Meaning |
|----------|---------|
| これはほんです。| This is a book. |
| たなかさんはせんせいです。| Tanaka-san is a teacher. |
| このりんごはおいしいです。| This apple is delicious. |

### Negative: では/じゃありません

To negate, replace です with **ではありません** (formal) or **じゃありません** (casual):

> これはほんではありません。= This is not a book.

> わたしはせんせいじゃないです。= I am not a teacher.

### Past tense of です

| Form | Japanese | Meaning |
|------|---------|---------|
| Present positive | 〜です | is/am/are |
| Present negative | 〜ではありません | is not |
| Past positive | 〜でした | was/were |
| Past negative | 〜ではありませんでした | was not |

## Basic Sentence Patterns

### Pattern 1: A は B です (A is B)

> わたし**は**がくせい**です**。= I am a student.
> かれ**は**いしゃ**です**。= He is a doctor.

### Pattern 2: A は B を V (A does B)

> わたしはほん**を**よみ**ます**。= I read a book.
> かれはみず**を**のみ**ます**。= He drinks water.

### Pattern 3: A は B に V (A goes to B)

> わたしはがっこう**に**いき**ます**。= I go to school.

### Pattern 4: A に B が あります/います

Use **あります** for non-living things, **います** for living things:

> つくえのうえにほんがあります。= There is a book on the desk.
> こうえんにこどもがいます。= There is a child in the park.

### Pattern 5: Adjective Sentences

> このへやはひろいです。= This room is spacious.
> そのえいがはおもしろくないです。= That movie is not interesting.

## Questions

Add **か** to the end of any sentence to make a question:

> これはほんです**か**？ = Is this a book?
> たなかさんはきます**か**？ = Will Tanaka-san come?

For **yes/no questions**:
- Yes = はい、そうです (Yes, that's right)
- No = いいえ、ちがいます (No, that's wrong/different)

For **question-word questions** (who, what, where, when, why, how):

| Question word | Hiragana | Meaning |
|--------------|---------|---------|
| 何 | なに/なん | what |
| 誰 | だれ | who |
| どこ | どこ | where |
| いつ | いつ | when |
| なぜ/どうして | なぜ/どうして | why |
| どうやって | どうやって | how |
| どれ | どれ | which |
| いくら | いくら | how much |

> どこにいきますか？ = Where are you going?
> なにをたべましたか？ = What did you eat?

## Topic vs Subject Revisited

One concept that trips up many learners:

> **わたしはがくせいです。** = I am a student. (は marks topic)

> **わたしがきました。** = *I* came. (が marks the specific subject — often used for emphasis or to answer "who?")

Think of it this way:
- **は (topic)**: "As for X, ..." — contextual, often old information
- **が (subject)**: "It is X who..." — emphasis, new information, answers a question

## Sentence-Final Particles

These softening particles come after the sentence-ending verb or です:

| Particle | Usage | Example |
|---------|-------|---------|
| ね | seeking agreement ("right?") | おいしいですね。(It's delicious, isn't it?) |
| よ | asserting new info ("you know") | これはほんですよ。(This is a book, I tell you.) |
| か | question | わかりましたか？(Did you understand?) |

## Conjunctions

To connect two sentences or ideas:

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| そして | soshite | and then |
| でも/けど | demo/kedo | but / however |
| だから | dakara | therefore / so |
| または | mata wa | or |

> ほんをよみます。そして、ねます。= I read a book. And then I sleep.

> にほんごはむずかしいです。でも、おもしろいです。= Japanese is difficult. But it's interesting.

## Counting Sentences

In Japanese, verbs agree with politeness level, not with person or number. The same verb form is used for I, you, he, she, we, they:

> わたしはたべます = I eat
> あなたはたべます = You eat
> かれらはたべます = They eat

This makes conjugation much simpler than many European languages.
