## JLPT N5 Practice Quiz

The JLPT N5 is the entry-level exam of the Japanese Language Proficiency Test. It tests:

- **Vocabulary** (語彙) — ~800 words
- **Grammar** (文法) — essential particles and sentence patterns
- **Kanji** (漢字) — ~80–100 characters
- **Reading comprehension** (読解)
- **Listening comprehension** (聴解) — not tested here

This quiz covers vocabulary, grammar, particles, kanji, and common expressions across 20 questions.

## What N5 Means

Passing JLPT N5 shows that you can:

- Read and understand typical expressions and sentences written in hiragana, katakana, and basic kanji
- Hear and comprehend conversations about familiar topics, spoken slowly
- Understand basic grammar patterns (です/ます, particles, simple conjugations)

The N5 passing threshold is **80/180 points** (around 44%), with separate passing criteria for vocabulary/grammar and listening.

## Your N5 Checklist

Use this as a self-assessment:

### Writing Systems
- [ ] Can read and write all 46 basic hiragana
- [ ] Can read and write all 46 basic katakana (including voiced/combos)
- [ ] Can recognize the ~80 essential N5 kanji

### Grammar
- [ ] Understand は, が, を, に, で, と, の, も, へ, か particles
- [ ] Can form polite present/past positive and negative verb forms (〜ます/〜ました/〜ません/〜ませんでした)
- [ ] Can conjugate い-adjectives (〜い, 〜くない, 〜かった, 〜くなかった)
- [ ] Can conjugate な-adjectives (〜です, 〜ではありません, 〜でした)
- [ ] Know the difference between います and あります
- [ ] Can use て-form for requests (〜てください) and progressive (〜ています)
- [ ] Understand basic sentence structure (SOV)

### Vocabulary
- [ ] Know ~800 common words (numbers, time, family, places, food, adjectives, verbs)
- [ ] Can understand and use essential daily phrases and greetings
- [ ] Know the days of the week and months
