## Why Greetings Matter

In Japanese culture, using the correct greeting at the correct time is important. Japanese greetings are **time-specific** and **context-sensitive** — there are different expressions for morning, afternoon, and evening, as well as distinctions between formal and casual speech.

## Daily Greetings

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning | When to use |
|----------|---------|---------|-------------|
| おはようございます | ohayou gozaimasu | Good morning (formal) | Before ~10am |
| おはよう | ohayou | Good morning (casual) | Friends/family |
| こんにちは | konnichiwa | Hello / Good afternoon | ~10am–6pm |
| こんばんは | konbanwa | Good evening | After ~6pm |
| おやすみなさい | oyasumi nasai | Good night (formal) | Before sleeping |
| おやすみ | oyasumi | Good night (casual) | Friends/family |

## Polite Expressions

These phrases are the building blocks of polite Japanese:

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| ありがとうございます | arigatou gozaimasu | Thank you (formal) |
| ありがとう | arigatou | Thank you (casual) |
| どうぞ | douzo | Please (offering something) / Go ahead |
| すみません | sumimasen | Excuse me / I'm sorry / Thank you |
| ごめんなさい | gomen nasai | I'm sorry (apology) |
| はい | hai | Yes |
| いいえ | iie | No |
| わかりました | wakarimashita | I understand / Got it |
| わかりません | wakarimasen | I don't understand |

> **すみません** is extremely versatile. Use it to get someone's attention (like a waiter), to apologize for a minor inconvenience, and even as a form of thanks for someone's trouble.

## Introductions

Meeting someone for the first time:

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| はじめまして | hajimemashite | Nice to meet you (first meeting) |
| わたしは〜です | watashi wa ~ desu | I am ~ |
| 〜からきました | ~ kara kimashita | I am from ~ |
| よろしくおねがいします | yoroshiku onegaishimasu | Please treat me well / Nice to meet you |
| おなまえは？ | onamae wa? | What is your name? |

### A Simple Introduction

> はじめまして。わたしはマリアです。アメリカからきました。よろしくおねがいします。

*Hajimemashite. Watashi wa Maria desu. Amerika kara kimashita. Yoroshiku onegaishimasu.*

"Nice to meet you. I am Maria. I am from America. I look forward to our relationship."

## Farewells

| Japanese | Reading | Meaning |
|----------|---------|---------|
| さようなら | sayounara | Goodbye (formal / final) |
| じゃあね | jaa ne | See you / Bye (casual) |
| またね | mata ne | See you later |
| またあした | mata ashita | See you tomorrow |
| いってきます | itte kimasu | I'm going (said when leaving home) |
| いってらっしゃい | itterasshai | Take care (response to いってきます) |
| ただいま | tadaima | I'm home |
| おかえり(なさい) | okaeri(nasai) | Welcome home |

The exchanges いってきます/いってらっしゃい and ただいま/おかえり are part of Japanese home culture — you'll encounter them constantly.

## Formal vs Casual Speech (敬語 vs タメ口)

Japanese has two main speech levels:

- **Polite (丁寧語, teineigo)**: Uses です/ます forms. Used with strangers, teachers, seniors.
- **Casual (タメ口, tameguchi)**: Drops です/ます. Used with close friends, family.

At the N5 level, **always use polite speech** unless you know someone very well. It is never wrong to be polite.
