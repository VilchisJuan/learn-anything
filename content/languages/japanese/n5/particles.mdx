## What Are Particles?

**Particles** (助詞, joshi) are small words that follow nouns and phrases to indicate their grammatical role in a sentence. They are one of the most important concepts in Japanese grammar.

In English, word order tells you who does what:
> "The cat eats the fish." vs "The fish eats the cat."

In Japanese, **particles** do this job — word order is flexible, but particles are essential:
> ねこがさかなをたべる。 = The cat eats the fish.
> さかなをねこがたべる。 = The cat eats the fish. (same meaning, different emphasis)

## The 10 Essential N5 Particles

### は (wa) — Topic Marker

**Marks the topic** of the sentence. Often translated as "as for..." or "speaking of..."

> わたし**は**がくせいです。= I am a student. (As for me, I am a student.)

> これ**は**ほんです。= This is a book.

Note: は is written with the hiragana for "ha" but pronounced **wa** when used as a particle.

---

### が (ga) — Subject Marker

**Marks the grammatical subject**. Often used when introducing new information, with adjectives expressing feelings/preferences, and when emphasizing the subject.

> ねこ**が**います。= There is a cat. / A cat is here.

> にほんご**が**すきです。= I like Japanese. (lit: Japanese is liked)

> だれ**が**きましたか？ = Who came?

**は vs が** is one of the most studied topics in Japanese grammar. Simply: は marks the topic (what you're talking about), が marks the subject (who/what does/is the action).

---

### を (wo) — Direct Object Marker

**Marks the direct object** — what the action is done to. Written を (wo) but pronounced **o**.

> ほん**を**よみます。= I read a book.

> みず**を**のみます。= I drink water.

> えいご**を**べんきょうします。= I study English.

---

### に (ni) — Location/Time/Destination

**Three main uses:**

1. **Destination** (with movement verbs):
   > がっこう**に**いきます。= I go to school.

2. **Specific time points**:
   > しちじ**に**おきます。= I wake up at 7 o'clock.
   > もくようび**に**かいます。= I'll buy it on Thursday.

3. **Indirect object** (recipient):
   > ともだち**に**てがみをかきます。= I write a letter to my friend.

---

### で (de) — Location of Action / Means

**Two main uses:**

1. **Location of action** (where something happens):
   > としょかん**で**べんきょうします。= I study at the library.

2. **Means/method** (how something is done):
   > でんしゃ**で**いきます。= I go by train.
   > にほんご**で**はなします。= I speak in Japanese.

**に vs で for location**: に marks where something *exists* (いる/ある). で marks where an *action takes place*.

---

### と (to) — And / With

**Two uses:**

1. **And** (connecting nouns):
   > りんご**と**バナナ = apple and banana

2. **With** (doing something together):
   > ともだち**と**えいがをみます。= I watch a movie with my friend.

---

### の (no) — Possessive / Noun Modifier

**Connects nouns** — similar to 's in English or "of":

> わたし**の**ほん = my book (my + book)
> にほん**の**くるま = Japanese car (Japan's car)
> だいがく**の**せんせい = university professor

---

### も (mo) — Also / Too

**Replaces は or が** to add "also/too":

> わたし**も**がくせいです。= I am also a student.

> これ**も**おいしいです。= This is also delicious.

With negative sentences, も means "either/neither":
> わたし**も**わかりません。= I don't understand either.

---

### へ (e/he) — Direction

**Marks direction of movement**. Similar to に but emphasizes the direction rather than the destination. Written へ (he) but pronounced **e** as a particle.

> とうきょう**へ**いきます。= I go toward/to Tokyo.

In casual modern Japanese, に and へ are often interchangeable for destinations.

---

### か (ka) — Question Marker

**Turns a statement into a question** — placed at the end of a sentence:

> これはほんです**か**？ = Is this a book?

> なんじですか？ = What time is it?

> たなかさんはきますか？ = Will Tanaka-san come?

No need to change word order for questions — just add か.

## Particle Quick Reference

| Particle | Function | Example |
|----------|---------|---------|
| は | Topic marker | わたし**は**〜 |
| が | Subject marker | ねこ**が**〜 |
| を | Object marker | ほん**を**よむ |
| に | Destination / Time / Indirect object | がっこう**に**いく |
| で | Location of action / Means | こうえん**で**あそぶ |
| と | And / With | ともだち**と**〜 |
| の | Possessive | わたし**の**〜 |
| も | Also / Too | わたし**も**〜 |
| へ | Direction | とうきょう**へ**〜 |
| か | Question marker | 〜ですか？ |
